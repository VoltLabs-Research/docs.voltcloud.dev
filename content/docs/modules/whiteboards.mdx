Whiteboards support freeform diagramming and note-taking within the team workspace.

![Whiteboards Overview](/assets/overviews/overviews_whiteboards.png)

## Listing and organization

The listing is a foldered document table: boards appear as title-based rows that you can open, rename, or delete. You can organize boards into folders, create and rename folders, move whiteboards between folders, and navigate the hierarchy through a breadcrumb at the top of the listing.

![Whiteboard Listing](/assets/modules/whiteboards/whiteboard-listing.png)

## Canvas experience

The editor is built on [Excalidraw](https://excalidraw.com/), an infinite-canvas model with shapes, arrows, text, freehand drawing, and background controls. Images are added through a dedicated **Insert image** menu item, or by pasting or dragging files onto the canvas, which uploads them as whiteboard assets. The main menu also offers **Save as image**, **Clear canvas**, **Toggle theme**, and **Change canvas background**.

## Real-time collaboration and sync behavior

Whiteboards synchronize board state in real time over Socket.io and show present collaborators. When you open a board, the client subscribes and receives a snapshot, then exchanges debounced delta patches as edits happen. Each board carries a monotonically increasing revision number, and clients send patches against the revision they last saw (`baseRevision`).

Conflicts are resolved automatically with no user-facing prompt: edits are merged per element using a last-writer-wins rule based on each element's version (falling back to its update timestamp and version nonce). There is no "keep local or apply remote" dialog.

## Persistence and storage

The scene state (elements and app state) and image assets are stored as objects in the team's storage cluster, while whiteboard metadata such as title, folder, and last editor lives in MongoDB. Edits are persisted on a short debounce after the in-memory board state changes.
